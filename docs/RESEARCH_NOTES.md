## Key lessons

1. **FX crosses 2015-2026 is a graveyard for non-momentum factors.** Carry, carry+trend, short-term mean reversion all produced negative Sharpe. Only slow momentum (XS-mom and TSMOM-LO) made money.

2. **Low correlation ≠ useful diversifier.** FX carry and FX MR both had correlation < 0.3 with XS-mom (mechanically independent), but negative Sharpe means blending them subtracts returns. Correlation alone doesn't justify inclusion; positive standalone Sharpe does.

3. **Negative IS-OOS degradation is not automatic good news.** Every strategy we tested had OOS Sharpe > IS Sharpe, driven by the 2023+ momentum regime. The honest long-run estimate is the IS number, not the OOS or full-sample.

4. **"Different mechanic, same universe" = same bet.** TSMOM and XS-mom are both momentum on the same instruments; correlation turned out to be +0.69 despite different math. To get real diversification, need either a different factor OR a different market (equity single-names, crypto perps, vol products).

5. **Research-to-live Sharpe degradation is CONFOUND-SPECIFIC, NOT a generic multiplier (rewritten 2026-05-22 — original framing was overgeneralization from a single data point).** The original 2026-05 framing — "plan for a 50-70% Sharpe haircut research → live" — was generalized from one experiment (`xs_momentum` QC port, research 0.92 → QC live 0.35) and has been **incorrectly propagating as a generic prior** into every subsequent strategy's expected-live-Sharpe section. The actual 0.57 absolute gap on xs_momentum had **three distinct, separable sources**:

   **(a) Universe-swap confound (~0.3 absolute Sh)**: research on Yahoo ETF D1 data, deploy on MT5 CFD universe. Different instruments (ETFs ≠ CFDs), different fundamental-data dependence, different liquidity profile, different overnight gap behavior.

   **(b) Cost-model gap (~0.10-0.15 absolute Sh)**: research at 0.01% commission (Yahoo D1 assumption), QC live at realistic ~5 bps RT.

   **(c) Sharpe-formula confound (~0.20-0.30 absolute Sh) — per lesson #21**: QC reports risk-free-rate-subtracted Sharpe; our research reports raw Sharpe. At ~4-5% rf 2022+, this alone subtracts ~0.2-0.3 absolute from a 5%/yr strategy. **Not a real edge degradation** — pure metric mismatch. Once both sides are compared on raw Sharpe (per the QC-equity-curve recomputation done 2026-04-19), the xs_momentum "haircut" shrinks meaningfully.

   (a) + (b) + (c) ≈ 0.5-0.7 absolute, matching the observed 0.57. **NONE of (a), (b), or (c) apply to the current 4-strategy book** (orb_dax, lunch_fade, xau_session, macro_drift), all of which:
   - Were researched on actual Eightcap CFD data (no universe swap → no confound a)
   - Used conservative cost models in research (typically 2× actual broker spread → confound b inverts; live is CHEAPER than research)
   - Compute Sharpe identically on research and live VPS reporting (confound c eliminated)

   **Corrected expectation for the current book: 10-25% RELATIVE haircut, probably under 0.20 absolute in most cases.** This is also consistent with how QC live haircuts work in published quant-fund letters when methodology is held constant across measurement.

   **What CAN still cause real Sharpe degradation live** (and where larger haircut priors ARE appropriate):
   - **Mechanism decay** (lesson #6, #28): the regime shifts post-validation. Real, observed on multiple repo strategies. Magnitude varies by mechanism.
   - **Sample-size variance at small live n**: at < 200 trades, σ(realized Sharpe) ≈ 0.7. A single year's "measured haircut" is dominated by sampling noise, not by underlying degradation.
   - **Operational drag**: slippage on stops, partial fills, broker re-quotes during illiquid moments. Typically 0.05-0.15 absolute Sh, less with sticky-order routing.
   - **Survivorship bias**: deploying strategies that *looked good* out of many tested. The 39:4 reject:deploy ratio in this repo helps but doesn't eliminate this.

   **Procedure for new deploys (replaces the old "plan for 50-70% haircut")**:
   1. Identify which of confounds (a)/(b)/(c) apply to THIS specific deploy. For the current pipeline (Eightcap research → Eightcap live), the answer is usually "none of them".
   2. Estimate operational drag from Phase 0 broker spread / fill behavior (lesson #32) — that's the relevant cost-model haircut.
   3. Treat first 12-24 months of live data as the empirical haircut measurement for THAT specific strategy on THIS account.
   4. Use the measured number going forward — not the xs_momentum-specific 0.57.

   **Addendum 2026-05-24 — event-driven multi-strategy books need a BIGGER haircut than the 10-25% relative for single-strategy clean deploys.** The 10-25% framing applies when (a) a single mechanism is being deployed (no selection bias), (b) the mechanism is regime-robust (pre/post sub-sample both work), and (c) the cost model is conservative. For an event-driven *book* assembled from 4+ candidates (FOMC + CPI + RS + NFP, after rejecting ECB + PPI), the realistic live haircut stacks:
   - **Selection bias from the 4-of-6 survivor pool**: -0.2 to -0.4 Sh
   - **Regime concentration on the 2 of 4 strategies (CPI, NFP) whose pre-2022 Sh is ~0**: -0.3 to -0.6 Sh per affected strategy
   - **Pre-event spread widening on real broker** (broker raises spread 5-10× of normal ~30s before scheduled high-impact macro releases — actual Eightcap behavior): -0.1 to -0.3 Sh
   - **Mechanism decay on the Lucca-Moench-family events** (academic-publication tail; original effect already 50% decayed per macro_drift W3→W4): -0.2 to -0.4 Sh

   Stacked, realistic event-book live Sh is **40-60% of research book Sh**, not the 10-25% haircut that applies to single-strategy intra-day mechanisms like orb_dax / lunch_fade. Per joint_book_audit.py 2026-05-24: research book Sh +3.2 (daily-aggregated, sparseness-inflated) → realistic live book Sh **+1.0 to +1.5**, not +2-3.

   **Discipline for event-driven books going forward**:
   - Per-event sizing: full risk on regime-robust events (pre-2022 Sh ≥ +0.30), half risk on regime-conditional events (pre-2022 Sh < +0.30).
   - Pre-commit a regime kill-switch: if NDX 60d realized vol < 12% AND Fed funds target unchanged for 6+ months, disable regime-conditional events.
   - Honest expected-live communication: lead with per-event Sh (~0.75) not annualized-by-sqrt(N) book Sh — the annualized number is mathematically correct but psychologically misleading for sparse-event strategies.
   - Per-event-class kill-trigger discipline (don't disable the whole book on one event-class failure).

   **Why this lesson was rewritten**: the original 50-70% framing was casting unjustified pessimism over every subsequent deploy. orb_dax has been live 30 days at apparent ~+2.5 Sharpe annualized (n=10, statistically meaningless but at least not failing the old "plan for 50% haircut" prediction by a worse-than-noise margin). The rewritten framing lets each new deploy be evaluated on its own empirical merits as live data accumulates, rather than anchoring against an irrelevant single-experiment number from a different research-to-live methodology. **Concretely: the appropriate expected-live-Sharpe haircut for the 4-strategy book is 10-25% relative, not 50-70%.**

6. **Regime decay ≠ bad strategy, but still a REJECT.** VIX term-structure VRP had Sharpe 1.14 in 2015-2017 (textbook VRP era), survived Volmageddon via backwardation filter, then died post-2022 (2024-2026 Sharpe -0.19). 0-DTE option flow + compressed VX curve appear to have thinned the underlying premium. Regime-dependent edges don't get a pass just because they "worked in the right era" — the forward-looking window is all that matters for deployment.

7. **Weight literature decay warnings heavily.** Equity pairs thesis cited the 1962-2002 Gatev/Goetzmann/Rouwenhorst paper AND the post-2002 decay research from Do & Faff (2010). Estimated Sharpe 0.4-0.7; actual -0.99. The decay warning deserved as much weight as the headline number — maybe more. When a mechanism's academic half-life has been measured, the recent number matters more than the canonical number. Mega-cap US pairs 2015-2026 is now a graveyard alongside FX non-momentum 2015-2026.

8. **"Active return over cash" can be tiny and the strategy still valuable as diversifier.** Treasury trend (IEF) gained +2.14%/yr vs BIL's +1.89%/yr — only +0.25%/yr active return. Standalone this is underwhelming. But it has Sharpe 0.54, MDD -9%, caught the 2022 bond crash (+1.41% vs TLT -29%), and has ≈ 0 correlation with XS-mom. Blended into a 70/30 XS-mom + IEF-trend book, you give up little return and gain meaningful DD-reduction in regimes where bonds work AND unchanged exposure when they don't. Diversifiers shouldn't be judged on standalone CAGR — they should be judged on whether they help the existing book in the regimes where the existing book hurts.

9. **Match filter speed to asset duration.** Same 252-day TSMOM signal on TLT (18y duration, ~15% vol) vs IEF (8y duration, ~7% vol) gave Sharpe 0.14 vs 0.54. TLT's vol creates 10-15% transitional drawdowns in the 6-month filter lag window that IEF's lower vol absorbs. When designing trend on a given instrument, the filter lookback must be proportional to the instrument's own vol — a rule that was implicit but not stated in the existing XS-mom / TSMOM work.

10. **The engine's permutation test is meaningless for continuous-position strategies.** `backtesting.statistics.permutation_test` shuffles the return series; permutation preserves mean and std exactly, so the null is mathematically identical to the observation. This works for discrete-trade strategies (flip trade PnL signs) but degenerates for continuous-weight strategies like TSMOM. For those, use a **position-shuffle** test: shuffle the daily weight series, preserve the actual return series, recompute P&L. This tests "does the timing of the position choices add value?" — the right null. Implemented inline in `experiments/treasury_trend/treasury_trend_validation.py`; consider promoting to `backtesting.statistics` if it comes up again.

11. **Extend the sample before accepting a marginal bootstrap result.** IEF Phase 3 bootstrap 95% CI on 11 years was [-0.028, +1.138] — a near-miss that would have tombstoned under strict reading. Extending to IEF's full 24-year history (2002-2026) moved the CI to [+0.26, +1.08] and raised Sharpe from 0.55 to 0.67. If the underlying instrument has earlier data and the mechanism is expected to work across pre-sample regimes, refetch before rejecting. An overfit strategy would LOSE Sharpe on extension; a real edge gains.

12. **CFD intraday costs + tick-volume ≠ cash-equity intraday.** ORB on SPX500 M5 2019-2026 failed at Sharpe -0.92 against Zarattini/Aziz (2023) reporting Sharpe 1.65-2.81 on QQQ. Key differences: (a) CFD spread ≈ 2× their commission assumption, (b) no real share volume for filters, (c) SPX500 is less momentum-driven than QQQ, (d) broader 30-min OR vs their 5-min first-bar OR. Published intraday results on cash equities do not port 1-1 to retail CFDs even when the mechanism nominally translates — friction + noise thresholds matter more than they do at daily cadence.

13. **Test the "fade" variant as a null check on any signal-based intraday strategy.** If both the signal and its mechanical opposite produce similar loss patterns (18% WR in both directions for ORB SPX500), the signal has no informational content — it is generating noise trades, not alpha trades. ~30 lines of code, saves days of tuning a dead mechanism.

14. **Stops inside typical intrabar range = instant death.** On SPX500 M5, a stop at 25-33% of OR width is inside typical single-bar noise envelope. Tightening stops dropped WR from 18% to 4%, far faster than it shrank avg loss. For any intraday strategy: measure intrabar range against planned stop distance BEFORE committing — if stop < 0.5 × intrabar ATR, expect >80% of trades to stop-out regardless of signal quality.

15. **Cross-instrument fade test separates signal from exit-structure artifact.** Running the same ORB mechanism on SPX500, NDX100, and GER40 exposed that GER40 had the highest absolute Sharpe (+0.38) but its fade variant was also positive (+0.34) — the edge was a structural R:R artifact (full-OR stop + EOD close = asymmetric book-keeping), not directional signal. NDX100 had weaker absolute Sharpe (+0.03) but the fade variant lost 0.46, proving real directional content. For cross-instrument strategies, the **fade-gap** (baseline Sharpe minus fade Sharpe) is a cleaner edge-quality indicator than absolute Sharpe. Numerically-best ≠ deployment-best.

16. **Recent-regime (holdout) sub-period is the honest deployment signal.** NDX100 baseline ORB had full-sample Sharpe +0.03, but the 2023-2026 holdout was the strongest window at +0.19. Adding trend-filter + tight-stop raised full-sample to +0.31 but made the holdout -0.19. When a "better" config has a WORSE holdout, the full-sample improvement is overfit noise, not real improvement. Prefer the baseline with the stronger holdout over the optimized variant with the weaker holdout — always.

17. **Don't tombstone based on fade-test alone — retest under symmetric R:R first.** GER40 ORB initially looked like a structural artifact (baseline Sharpe +0.38, fade +0.34, small gap). The refinement run exposed that the EOD-close exit was giving both directions the SAME R:R asymmetry (fixed loss cap + random EOD distribution), which inflated fade's Sharpe by coincidence. Under fixed 1:1 R:R exits, baseline Sh -0.24 vs fade -1.21, gap +0.97 — real directional signal. **Rule**: when baseline and fade converge under asymmetric exits, rerun both under SYMMETRIC R:R before concluding the signal is vestigial. EOD-exit with fixed-distance stop is itself a confound.

18. **Time-of-day exit as an alpha discovery tool.** GER40 ORB refinement: full-sample Sharpe at EOD exit was +0.38; replacing with T+180min exit jumped to +0.58 with tighter DD. The edge in opening-impulse momentum has a half-life of about 3 hours on DAX M5 — holding longer accumulates noise. For any intraday strategy where you're exiting at EOD "because that's what the literature does", test a T+60/120/180/240min sweep — often the edge is concentrated in early bars and diluted by holding till close.

19. **Refinement paths are instrument-specific, not universal.** The T+180min exit that unlocked GER40's edge (Sh +0.38 → +0.58) was tested on NDX100 and FAILED decisively — NDX100's optimum is actually the LONGEST hold (EOD-exit Sharpe +0.03 beats T+180 of -0.09). Under tight R:R exits on NDX100, fade-variant beats baseline (-0.17 gap at 1:1 R:R) — opposite of GER40 where baseline dominates. Different instruments have different post-breakout autocorrelation profiles (DAX has clean 3h opening-impulse momentum; NDX has weak full-session drift + short-term mean reversion). When investigating a new instrument, run the symmetric-R:R-plus-TOD-sweep diagnostic from scratch — do NOT assume the previous instrument's refinement ports over.

20. **"Marginal" strategies don't become non-marginal through refinement.** NDX100 baseline was always the ceiling, not a floor — every refinement variant produced lower regime-averaged Sharpe. Lead variants that looked good in 2019-20 collapsed in 2021-26. Holdout-degradation was the kill signal each time: the "improved" variants had worse 2023-2026 holdout Sharpe than the baseline they replaced. If refinement hurts the modern-regime sub-period while "improving" the full-sample number, you're overfitting early-sample regimes. Prefer baseline.

21. **QC's Sharpe is risk-free-adjusted; our research Sharpe is raw.** First observed on Treasury-trend QC port 2026-04-19: research Sharpe 0.42 (OOS 2015-2026), QC reported Sharpe -0.263 on same period. Strategy wasn't broken — QC's formula subtracts ~3% annual risk-free rate; our `annualized_sharpe()` does not. Raw Sharpe computed from the QC equity curve (CAGR 2.26% / annualized std 3.4% = 0.66) matched research 0.67 almost exactly. For every future Phase 8 backtest: **don't compare QC-reported Sharpe directly to research-reported Sharpe**. Either compute the raw version from QC's equity output, or add risk-free-subtraction to our research sim. The comparison we actually care about (edge after realistic costs) needs the same formula on both sides. Corollary: at high rf (~4-5% post-2022), any strategy returning < 5%/year will have negative QC-reported Sharpe even if positive-returning. This is structural, not pathological.

22. **CFD overnight/gap theses must be validated on real futures BEFORE refinement work.** DAX overnight drift looked like a +0.80 Sharpe research result on MT5 CFD; FDAX futures via QC gave Sh -0.34 — a complete inversion. The CFD vendor's overnight pricing carries broker-specific synthetic mark-to-market that does not exist in real futures. Before sinking refinement time into any CFD-data thesis whose mechanism centres on the overnight/auction transition, port the baseline to QC futures and confirm directional sign matches. Costs more time upfront, saves more downstream when the CFD artifact is exposed.

23. **Pre-close MOC drift requires public imbalance publication + single-venue concentrated basket.** Tested on NYSE (SPX), Nasdaq (NDX), and Xetra (DAX). DAX has no public imbalance feed during continuous trading — mechanism cannot operate; result sign-inverted. SPX has imbalance feed but basket is split NYSE/Nasdaq, so signal dilutes — no directional content. NDX satisfies both (dir-gap +0.74) but gross effect ~4-5 bps per trade is fully consumed by 1pt CFD round-trip. **Microstructure prerequisite framework** for any "public-info intraday drift" thesis: require (a) public publication during continuous trading AND (b) tradeable basket's constituents concentrated on that one venue. If either fails, don't even spin up the thesis at M5 CFD friction.

24. **Generic intraday patterns get arbed on NDX M5; time-of-day structural theses can extract edge.** Four generic-trigger NDX M5 strategies (ORB continuation, z-score fade, BB-band fade, pre-close MOC drift) all REJECT despite real directional content in null-check diagnostics. But the time-of-day-structural lunch-hour fade PASSES decisively (Sh +0.89, holdout +1.06, dir-gap +1.87, cost-insensitive). The difference: lunch fade fires only on outlier-magnitude days (filter selects ~28 days/year), per-trade gross is 70-80 bps which dwarfs 2-5 bps CFD spread. Friction is overcome by signal magnitude, not by signal frequency. **For future NDX M5 work**: don't propose another generic-pattern variant; do consider further time-of-day-structural theses (pre-FOMC drift, post-open vol-crunch fade, end-of-month rebalance, futures-roll-day microstructure).

25. **"Holdout is the best regime" is the strongest possible Phase-6 outcome.** Most repo strategies show post-2022 decay (best pre-2022, worst post). NDX lunch fade is the inverse: 2019-2020 +0.61, 2021-2022 +1.00, 2023-2026 holdout +1.06. The mechanism may have INTENSIFIED with 0DTE-options-related intraday chop. When a holdout outperforms in-sample, weight the result more, not less — the deploy decision is forward-looking and the holdout is the most recent forward-look you have.

26. **Cost-insensitivity Sharpe vs cost-linear Sharpe is diagnostic.** When cost drag is ~0.04 Sharpe per pt RT (NDX lunch fade), per-trade gross dwarfs friction and the edge is robust to retail spread variation. When drag is ~0.16 Sharpe per pt RT (SPX lunch fade) or higher (~2.3 Sh/pt on SPX pre-close), per-trade gross is small and the friction is the binding constraint. **Cost-zero Sharpe extrapolation** separates "no edge" (cost-zero ≈ 0) from "edge eaten by friction" (cost-zero >> 0, cost-linear collapse) — different mechanistic stories, both REJECTs at retail M5 CFD, but the diagnostic guides where to look next (lower-cost execution venue for the latter, different mechanism for the former).

27. **Lunch fade is a structural exception, not a generic "fade overshoot on indices" rule.** Three independent fade-thesis attempts on retail-M5 indices have now sign-inverted: DAX gap fade (continuation +0.12 / fade -1.04), DAX US-lead (continuation -0.07 / fade -0.48), VWAP fade NDX (continuation +0.13 / fade -0.51). The 11:30-13:30 ET lunch window's success at fading morning moves cannot be generalised to "late session" or "session deviation" frameworks. The mechanism that makes lunch-fade work is SPECIFICALLY the simultaneous NY/Chicago lunch + EU close = institutional flow vacuum, leaving HFT inventory rebalancing dominant (mean-reverting). Outside that exact confluence, the late-session US-index tape trends, doesn't revert — and the trend bias has intensified post-2022 with 0DTE-options gamma flow. For future "fade overshoot" theses: require explicit identification of the liquidity-vacuum mechanism, not just "price has deviated from some reference."

28. **Holdout regime decay magnitude as a 0DTE-amplification proxy.** Strategies that mean-revert intraday on US indices show severe holdout decay post-2022: VWAP fade NDX +0.06 (2019-20) → -1.27 (2023-26), VWAP fade SPX +0.06 → -1.41. The lunch-fade is the ONLY repo strategy where post-2022 INTENSIFIED the mechanism (+0.61 → +1.06). The diagnostic: if the mechanism predicts mean-reversion AND 2023-26 is the worst regime by >0.5 Sharpe vs 2019-20, expect 0DTE-options trend-amplification to be the killer. If 2023-26 is the BEST regime, the mechanism is robust to or aided by 0DTE microstructure.

31. **BTC deploy-discipline (2026-05-13): W4 (post-institutionalization) Sharpe must be the binding constraint on any new BTC daily thesis.** Three BTC theses closed on 2026-05-13 (btc_trend retired on decay; btc_weekend rejected — activation but full-sample MDD fails; btc_volbreak rejected — one-window wonder in W2 2020-2021). Together they map the institutionalization phase transition from three different mechanism families. The deploy-relevant question is no longer "what's the full-sample Sharpe?" — it's "does the mechanism work in the post-institutionalization regime (CME volume scaled, Q1 2024 spot ETFs, professional 24/7 desks)?". **Rule for future BTC daily theses**: pre-commit a W4 Sharpe floor (e.g., +0.30 at honest costs, after the 0.3-0.6 research-to-live haircut from lesson #5) as a binding kill criterion, set tight enough that a W2-only-driven full-sample pass cannot survive. Strategies whose edge is concentrated in 2018-2021 are not deployable, period — even if the mechanism is "real" in a backtest sense. This is the BTC-specific instance of the more general "holdout regime is the honest deployment signal" rule (lesson #16), tightened from "best regime is informative" to "post-institutionalization regime is binding".

30. **BTC institutionalization is a mirror-image driver across thesis families.** Same maturation of BTC market structure (CME futures volume scaling 2018+, Q1 2024 spot ETF approval, professional 24/7 desks) acts on different mechanisms in opposite directions:
    - **Slow trend continuation (btc_trend MH-TSMOM)**: institutional flow smooths trend persistence → W4 Sh +0.50 vs +1.38/+1.61 earlier; W4 Sharpe roughly halves. EDGE DEGRADES with institutionalization.
    - **Weekend microstructure asymmetry (btc_weekend continuation)**: institutional Monday re-engagement vs retail weekend tape CREATES the asymmetry → W1+W2 Sh -0.01/-0.17 (mechanism dormant) → W3+W4 +2.11/+1.81 (mechanism activated). EDGE ACTIVATES with institutionalization.
    Both observed 2026-05-13 on the same 8-year BTCUSD D1 window. The institutionalization narrative is no longer a single-thesis artifact — it's a structural property of post-2022 BTC corroborated by two independent strategy outcomes pointing opposite directions. **For future BTC theses**: pre-commit on which side of institutionalization the mechanism lives. Slow trend / momentum / autocorrelation theses inherit the decay; microstructure-asymmetry / counterparty-driven / 24/7-vs-5d theses inherit the activation. Cross-corollary: a btc_weekend variant filtered to post-2022 with explicit regime-detection on the activation side is a candidate Phase 1 thesis worth scoping, but only as a separate experiment with its own pre-committed criteria — not as a refinement of btc_weekend (per lumber_oats lesson).

29. **Walk-forward Phase 6 surfaces structural failure modes that single-split Phase 6 hides.** BTC trend single-split Phase 6 (IS 2018-2021 / OOS 2022-2025) failed degradation by 0.04 — a borderline miss that looked like split-lottery noise. Walk-forward across 5 rolling 3y-IS/2y-OOS splits showed mean degradation +0.548 with **two outlier splits (+1.70 and +1.13) both at parabola-top → V-bottom transitions** (2021→2022 and 2025→2026). Two independent observations of the same failure mode = structural weakness of slow-TSMOM (still long at the top, slow to flip on sharp reversals), not lottery. 59. **CFD swap-cost ceiling makes multi-day-hold equity strategies structurally non-deployable on retail CFD brokers (2026-05-24).** `pead_midcap` is the load-bearing example: Phase 2 per-event PEAD PASSES every commission-only kill criterion (Sh +0.76, dir-gap +1.71, 3/3 regimes, cost-insensitive to 30bp commission), but **adding the realistic CFD swap cost destroys it**. Eightcap (and CFD brokers generally) charge ~6.5–8.5% annualized financing on long stock-CFD positions; shorts pay the negative leg AND don't refund the carry. For a 20-business-day hold at ~7% carry: swap cost ≈ 7% × 20/252 = **55 bp per side**, or **~110 bp round-trip** on a long-short basket. PEAD per-event gross is 100–200 bp — swap eats more than half, taking live Sharpe to ~0 or negative. This was NOT in the pre-commit cost model (which only tested commission); swap is a structural CFD-broker cost that does not appear in equity-cash backtests and was incorrectly omitted.

   **Mandatory Phase 0 gate going forward — for ANY multi-day-hold thesis (HOLD ≥ 2 days) on this CFD book**:
   ```
   swap_cost_budget_rt = (broker_carry_pct + 2.5%) × hold_days / 252 × 2
   require: backtest_gross_per_trade > 1.5 × swap_cost_budget_rt
   ```
   At ~7% carry:
   - HOLD=1d → swap_budget ≈ 5.6 bp → gross needed > 8 bp (trivial)
   - HOLD=5d → swap_budget ≈ 28 bp → gross needed > 42 bp (manageable)
   - HOLD=10d → swap_budget ≈ 56 bp → gross needed > 84 bp (tight)
   - HOLD=20d → swap_budget ≈ 110 bp → gross needed > 165 bp (most equity-drift mechanisms fail this)
   - HOLD=60d → swap_budget ≈ 330 bp → gross needed > 495 bp (only the strongest trend-following clears)

   **Strategies in the existing repo this constraint already explains**: the 4 deployed strategies (orb_dax / lunch_fade / xau_session / macro_drift) and 1 candidate (pre_cpi_drift) are all single-day or intraday — no swap. The KEEP-FOR-REFERENCE multi-day strategies (`tsmom`, `btc_trend`, `btc_intraday`) are positioned where multi-day gross is large enough or live trading isn't on Eightcap CFDs. `treasury_trend` / `softs_ensemble` are blocked at instrument access, but even if those broker-blockers cleared, swap-cost-vs-gross would need re-checking.

   **What this rules out vs leaves open**:
   - Rules OUT on current CFD book: classical multi-day PEAD, multi-week mean reversion, slow-trend equity ensembles, long-hold gap-continuation, anything with HOLD > ~10 days on stock CFDs.
   - Leaves OPEN: same-day / intraday strategies (no swap), short-hold (≤ 5d) strategies with gross > 50 bp, multi-day strategies on FX (negative-carry pairs can earn swap), multi-day on indexes (lower carry than single stocks), or any strategy reformulated for cash-equity execution at a prime broker (IBKR margin, Schwab, etc.) where 20d holds are essentially free of financing.
   - Future thesis prompts implicitly screened by this: don't propose "trade earnings drift over 4-6 weeks" or "weekly XS basket rotation" on the CFD-only book; either pivot to short-hold variant, or flag as "cash-equity-only" upfront in the thesis.

63. **Absolute-ATR (or any absolute-vol-derived) filter on a secular-drift instrument is mechanically equivalent to a year-filter, not a vol-filter (2026-05-25).** Surfaced jointly by `xau_imbalance_m15` REJECT and `xau_break_retest_m15` Phase 2. Both demos pre-committed an "ATR(14) at signal-bar > X USD" filter to capture the user's discretionary heuristic ("ATR > 8-10 tradeable, < 4-5 skip"). On XAU's 2018-2026 sample, the ATR(14) distribution shifts monotonically upward — driven by the underlying level rising ~1800 → ~5000+ USD (XAU is up 3× over the window, and absolute ATR scales roughly with price for percentage-vol-stable instruments). Result: **any "ATR > 5 USD" floor mechanically selects post-2022 bars** at ~90% concentration. On `xau_imbalance_m15`, ATR-floor variants 5/7/10 dropped W1 (2018-2020) sample to 4-18 trades and W2 (2021-2022) to 0-5 trades — INSUFFICIENT_N — while the W3 (2023-2026) sample stayed at 100+ trades and showed apparent "regime-isolation lift" of +1.30. On `xau_break_retest_m15`, the same artifact: all 4 ATR-floor variants (3/5/7/10) collapsed W1+W2 to n<10, while W3 retained 60-200 trades. The "ATR isolates vol regime" intuition is correct in principle but **broken in practice** for instruments whose price level has trended significantly over the sample window.

   **Generic rule for any vol-derived filter on a secular-drift instrument**: do NOT use absolute thresholds (ATR > X USD, range > Y pips, etc.). Use one of:
   - **Percentile-of-trailing-N**: `ATR_today > percentile(ATR_trailing_252d, 50)` — bins by within-year vol regime, not cross-year price level
   - **Price-normalized vol**: `(ATR_today / close_today) > X%` — strips out the level-trend
   - **Z-score-of-trailing-N**: `(ATR_today - mean(trailing)) / std(trailing) > 1.0` — same as percentile but parametric

   **Affected instruments to pre-flag**: XAU 2018-2026 (3×), BTC 2017-2026 (multi-decade), NDX/QQQ tech 2010-2026 (5×), MES/SPX 2009-2026 (4×), Russell 2009-2026 (3×), most single-stock CFDs over multi-year windows. **NOT affected**: range-bound FX crosses (EURUSD has stable absolute range), VIX index (stationary by construction), stable-fundamentals commodities like agricultural softs at multi-year horizons.

   **Discovery context**: both M15 follow-up experiments (imbalance + break-retest) used the user's discretionary ATR rule as a pre-commit. The user's heuristic was *correct in spirit* (skip dead-vol days) but absolute-threshold implementation captures the wrong axis on XAU. The same heuristic would work cleanly on relative-ATR terms. **For the discretionary record**: the user is unknowingly running a within-day-relative vol filter (his eye anchors today's ATR against recent days/weeks, not against the absolute price level) — that's why it works for him but breaks when literalized as `ATR_USD > 5` over a 7.9y backtest sample.

   **Companion to lesson #5 (research-to-live haircut framing)**: this is another instance of a heuristic that works at the trader's experience-window timescale but fails when ported to a longer backtest window where the underlying regime has shifted. Any future "user-discretionary-rule → systematic-filter" port must validate that the rule's mathematical formulation is stationary across the full backtest sample, not just within the trader's recent experience.
