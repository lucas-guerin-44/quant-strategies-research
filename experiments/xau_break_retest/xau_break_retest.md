## Thesis (mechanism)

1. **Liquidity pools at recent swing highs/lows.** In M5 timeframe on XAU
   during NY hours, the last 20-40 bars typically contain a visible swing
   high and swing low marking where stop-loss orders (resting + bracket) and
   pending-breakout orders accumulate.
2. **A close beyond the swing level triggers a stop-run cascade.** Resting
   stops fire, momentum traders chase, the break is mechanically amplified
   for 2-5 bars beyond the level. This is the "break" leg.
3. **First pullback retests the broken level.** After the initial impulse,
   short-term mean-reversion traders (and partial-fill liquidity providers)
   pull price back toward the broken level. If the level was a real
   information event (stops cleared, new directional belief), price holds
   above (for an upside break) and continuation resumes. If the break was
   noise, price closes back through and the move fails.
4. **The retest is the entry trigger because it filters noise breaks.**
   Entering on the close-beyond-level produces too many failed breakouts;
   waiting for the retest-and-hold filters out roughly half (the failed
   ones close back through during the retest) while sacrificing only the
   initial 2-5 bar impulse.
5. **NY-cash-open session timing is structural.** Comex futures roll over
   into US session liquidity; ETF flows concentrate; US-macro releases
   (10:00 ET data, 11:00 OPEC headlines, FOMC) all fire in the 09:00-12:00
   ET window. The break+retest pattern works when there's a real
   information catalyst; off-hours the same pattern is just noise (per the
   XAU H1 hour-of-day profile: NY hours have weaker directional drift than
   Asia hours per `xau_session/_profile_xau_hod.py`, but realized vol is
   much higher — better R:R per trade even if per-bar drift is lower).

  a shorter time-exit (T+60 default) because XAU M5 NY-session momentum
  half-life is shorter than DAX morning-session momentum.

## Signal math — pre-commit pseudo-code

```
Parameters (5 hard cap):
  SWING_LOOKBACK_BARS    = 20    (recent swing high/low over last N M5 bars)
  RETEST_WINDOW_BARS     = 4     (must retest within M bars after break)
  RETEST_TOL_ATR         = 0.30  (retest = price within tol*ATR of level)
  SESSION_START_UTC      = 13    (13:00 UTC, ~09:00 ET, NY cash open)
  SESSION_END_UTC        = 16    (16:00 UTC, ~12:00 ET, end of first 2.5h)
  STOP_ATR_MULT          = 1.20  (stop = level +/- mult*ATR(14), small buffer)

Derived:
  ATR_PERIOD             = 14   (M5 ATR, standard)
  TIME_EXIT_MIN          = 60   (3-12 M5 bars typical hold; flat at 60 min)
  ENTRY_CUTOFF_UTC       = 15   (no NEW entries after 15:00 UTC; manage open)

Per M5 bar b in session [13:00, 16:00) UTC, time-anchored on the UTC date:

  # 1. Identify swing levels
  swing_high = max(high[b-SWING_LOOKBACK : b])
  swing_low  = min(low[b-SWING_LOOKBACK : b])
  atr        = ATR14[b-1]   (use closed bars only, no look-ahead)

  # 2. Detect break (an M5 bar close beyond the swing level)
  if (flat) and (b.close > swing_high) and (no break already today):
    mark "UP break" at index i_break = b
  elif (flat) and (b.close < swing_low) and (no break already today):
    mark "DOWN break" at index i_break = b

  # 3. Within RETEST_WINDOW bars of i_break, look for retest
  # UP retest: bar low touches within RETEST_TOL_ATR * atr above swing_high
  # AND the bar's close stays above swing_high (no invalidation)
  for k in [i_break+1, i_break+RETEST_WINDOW]:
    if UP break:
      if (low[k] <= swing_high + RETEST_TOL_ATR * atr) and (close[k] > swing_high):
        enter LONG at close[k]
        stop = swing_high - STOP_ATR_MULT * atr
        time_exit = entry_ts + TIME_EXIT_MIN
        break
      if close[k] < swing_high:
        invalidate (close back through level)
        break
    if DOWN break:
      symmetric (mirror conditions)

  # 4. Exit on first of:
  #   - stop hit (bar.low <= stop for LONG; bar.high >= stop for SHORT)
  #   - time exit (entry_ts + TIME_EXIT_MIN)
  #   - session end (16:00 UTC flat regardless)
  # No take-profit. R is set by stop only; let winners run to time-exit.

  # 5. Max 1 round-trip per day per direction (prevents double-counting same break).
```

**Free parameters (counted):** SWING_LOOKBACK_BARS, RETEST_WINDOW_BARS,
RETEST_TOL_ATR, STOP_ATR_MULT, SESSION window (start+end as one). = **5
total, at the cap.** TIME_EXIT_MIN, ATR_PERIOD, and ENTRY_CUTOFF_UTC are
fixed by convention — they are NOT swept.

## Why this might fail (red flags)

1. **Retest-and-continuation is one of the most-published retail patterns.**
   Volman, ICT, Wyckoff, supply-and-demand — all teach variants of this.
   Likely arbed down on XAU specifically, which is the most retail-traded
   instrument after EURUSD.
2. **XAU NY-session is news-dominated, not microstructure-dominated.** US
   CPI / NFP / FOMC fires in this exact window. A clean swing-break+retest
   often gets blown through by a single news bar regardless of pattern.
   The strategy could be PnL-driven by 10-15 outlier news days and noise
   on the rest.
3. **Stops at 1.2 × ATR are inside typical news-event intra-bar range.**
   Per ORB SPX500 lesson (RESEARCH_NOTES #14), if stop < 0.5 × intrabar
   ATR, > 80% stop-out rate. M5 ATR ~3 points on XAU normal day, but
   news-event M5 bars can be 20-50 points — single bar takes out the
   stop AND the time-exit instantly.
4. **The discretionary trade history is SHORT-skewed in a bull market.**
   If the systematized rule turns out to also work better short, that's
   either a regime artifact (post-2024 NY-open selling pressure) or a
   genuine session-asymmetric edge. Either way, the long-only variant
   may underperform symmetric — different from `xau_session` which is
   long-only by design (Asia-open structural demand).
5. **Discretionary WR 65% sets an unrealistically high bar.** A
   1-minute-hold scalper sees a different P&L distribution than a
   M5-systematic. Don't anchor on 65% — pre-commit 35-50% WR expectation.

## Mechanistic interpretation — why continuation fails on XAU M5 NY-session

The break-retest *pattern* exists on XAU M5 — the simulator finds 200+
qualifying setups per year, so the structural ingredients (recent swing, M5
break, retest within 4 bars without invalidation) are real. The directional
sign of the resolution is the problem.

Three non-exclusive mechanistic drivers explain the inversion:

1. **XAU NY-session is news-driven, not order-flow-driven.** Per the
   `xau_session/_profile_xau_hod.py` cross-window analysis, hour 13-16 UTC
   carries the bulk of US-macro releases (CPI 12:30 UTC = 08:30 ET, FOMC
   14:00 ET / 18:00 UTC, NFP 12:30 UTC). A break-on-news is an information
   shock that almost always *overshoots* (initial-reaction trades, then
   institutional fade as the headline gets digested). The retest is the
   fade — and entering long ON the retest catches the *bottom* of the
   institutional fade, not the resumption. Continuation traders are
   buying the second-fastest-mover after the initial shock; the
   retest-fader catches the third leg (the eventual revert).
2. **The retest is a liquidity-event endpoint, not a confirmation.** On
   M5 bars, the retest is where market makers re-anchor after the
   initial impulse; their order flow concentrates *against* the impulse
   direction (they sold into the up-break and are now buying back at the
   retest to cover, which puts upward pressure that fades as the cover
   completes). The continuation entry buys at the peak of the
   cover-pressure, then watches the fade-back resume.
3. **XAU 2018-2026 NY-session is mean-reverting in M5, not trending.**
   This contradicts the user's intuition (and the popular retail teaching
   on ICT/Wyckoff continuation patterns) but matches the `xau_session`
   cross-window finding that the Asian-overnight session has structural
   drift while NY-session does NOT — its W1 mean is +0.001%, W2 -0.005%,
   W4 -0.004% on H1 bars. Drift-of-the-hour is the necessary condition
   for any continuation strategy; absent drift, every M5 retest is a
   mean-reversion opportunity.

### Why is `long-only` the best of a bad bunch?

The long-only variant (Sh -0.25, holdout +0.05, MDD only -9%) is the *least*
broken — its holdout is marginally positive. This is the *gold bull-market
drift* leaking through: on every up-break-retest day, the strategy buys, and
the average XAU day in 2024-2026 closes higher than its open. The mechanism
isn't working; the long-side drift is dragging the long-only result up.

This is the same dynamic that caught `pre_xau_macro_drift` (RESEARCH_NOTES
references the "gold-bull drift coincidentally sampled on event days"
confound — same shape here). The long-only +0.05 holdout is the *placebo*
strength, not the *strategy's* strength.

### What the experiment confirms (negative deliverable)

1. **The "break of structure + retest = continuation" retail teaching does
   NOT work on XAU M5 NY-session 2018-2026.** Across 5 free parameters and
   6 pre-committed variants, no configuration produces deployable edge.
2. **The mechanism's *opposite* (fade the retest) has weak-positive
   signal**, especially on wide-tolerance retests (gap -0.97, FADE Sh
   +0.13). Possible Phase 1 candidate for a separate "retest-fade"
   thesis, but the absolute Sharpe is still too thin for direct deploy

3. **The discretionary trade history's SHORT skew (61%) is partly
   *correctly* identifying that NY-session XAU intraday has a fade bias**,
   but the user achieves it via 1-minute-hold scalps with WR 65%, not via
   any pattern-based entry. Systematizing a sub-minute scalping rule from
   M5 OHLC isn't possible (sub-bar dynamics are invisible at M5
   resolution).

### Connection to the existing book

`xau_session` (DEPLOYED_PAPER, Asia-overnight) and this experiment are NOT
the same trade applied to different sessions — they are *opposite-direction*
trades. `xau_session` rides Asia-open structural physical/sovereign demand;
this experiment tested whether NY-session momentum continues post-retest and
the answer is no, NY-session XAU M5 mean-reverts. The two findings are
complementary: XAU is a *driver-asymmetric instrument* where overnight flow
drives trends and US-session flow drives reversions.

### What was NOT tested (deliberate scope cap, not gaps)

- **News-event filter** (skip ±60 min around US-macro releases). Not tested
  because the strategy is broken across regimes well outside event windows
  too (W1 2019-2020 Sh -2.05 is structural, not event-driven).
- **Higher TF (M15, H1)** — the user trades M5 primarily, so M5 was the
  thesis. M15 might dampen the news-noise but would also widen the stop
  beyond useful R:R.
- **Volume filter** — XAU CFD tick-volume is not real volume (RESEARCH_NOTES
  #12); filtering by it would chase ghosts.
- **Volatility regime gate** (high-VIX vs low-VIX) — out of scope for the
  ≤5-parameter cap pre-committed in the thesis.

The experiment has done its job: documented a clean REJECT with mechanistic
explanation. Tombstoned.

### Lessons (for RESEARCH_NOTES.md candidates)

- **Retail break-of-structure + retest continuation does NOT systematize on
  XAU M5 NY-session.** Fade-gap is uniformly negative across 5 variants.
  Combined with `orb` finding that ORB-style level-continuation fails on
  multi-venue / news-driven sessions, the pattern is: continuation-on-level
  works only on *single-venue concentrated-auction* setups (DAX Xetra open)
  where the level is a real microstructure artifact, not a chart-shape
  artifact. XAU NY-session has neither single venue nor concentrated
  auction.
- **Discretionary high-WR scalping (65% WR at 1-min holds) does NOT imply
  a M5-systematic edge exists.** The user's profitability operates at a
  resolution invisible to M5 OHLC. Future "systematize the user's
  discretionary book" theses must check TF compatibility before
  committing.
